Studio Ghibli

Studio Ghibli is a Japanese animation studio that was founded in Tokyo in 1985. The studio's name comes from the Arabic name for "hot sahara wind". The founders, Isao Takahata, Toshio Suzuki, and Hayao Miyazaki, wanted to bring new wind to the anime industry.


Studio Ghibli has produced 20 feature films, several short films, and television commercials. Their films include Porco Rosso, Whisper of the Heart, and Princess Mononoke. They have also created two television films.


Studio Ghibli is often called Japan's answer to Disney. They have introduced many people to animation and have produced some of Japan's highest-grossing films.


The studio's films often have themes of environmentalism, friendship, love, family, culture, Japanese history, war, and human emotions.
